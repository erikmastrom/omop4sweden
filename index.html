Making OMOP a formal part of the Swedish health data ecosystem

Mobilizing for an increased use of the international data model OMOP, to unlock the potential of Swedish health data through efficient and effective secondary use.

More about OMOP 4 Sweden!

OMOP 4 Sweden! brings together a number of organizations in Swedish life science and healthcare who want to help Sweden build a competitive ability to effectively use our health data assets to generate valuable evidence for better healthcare. We intend to take important steps to create the necessary commitment and improved conditions for increased standardisation of Swedish health data, enabling effective secondary use.

OMOP in Sweden

Below are some of the early adopters in Sweden who either use OMOP, OHDSI, and federated analysis themselves, or support its establishment in the Swedish health data ecosystem. More about OMOP in Sweden
The Government Offices
Assignment to the National Board of Health and Welfare to investigate the adaptation of health data registries to OMOP standards

The Swedish Government has tasked the National Board of Health and Welfare with investigating and describing the conditions, costs, and benefits of enabling the release of data from health registries in accordance with the Observational Medical Outcomes Partnership Common Data Model (OMOP) in order to facilitate international collaboration and research.

Karolinska Institutet - MEB
Implemented OMOP for data on older adults in Sweden

KI MEB has implemented OMOP for datasets focused on pharmacoepidemiology and medication use, and is a data partner to the EHDEN Foundation.

University of Gothenburg / SCIFI-PEARL
Largest and most active OMOP implementation in Sweden

SCIFI-PEARL is a national linked multi-register study with regular updates and is a data partner in both EHDEN and DARWIN-EU.

SWIBREG
Swedish Inflammatory Bowel Disease Registry

SWIBREG is mapped to OMOP through EHDEN and contributes as a data partner to the EHDEN Foundation.

Uppsala Monitoring Center
Early OMOP adopter in pharmacovigilance

UMC has used OMOP and federated networks for Real-World Evidence in signal management within pharmacovigilance.

Lund University, Intelligent Systems Laboratory
AI-based decision support for colon cancer care

Contributes to FLORENCE with trustworthy AI and OMOP-based privacy-preserving federated learning.
